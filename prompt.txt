Context: Analyze US financial data from the last 24 hours, specifically focusing on, but not limiting to: 1. Major Indices (S&P 500/VIX), 2. Bond Yields (10-Year Treasury), and 3. Top Headlines from major financial outlets (WSJ, Bloomberg, CNBC).

Task: Score the Market Sentiment (Bullish vs. Bearish) across exactly these 5 dimensions.

The Scale: Use a scale of -5 to +5.
* -5 = Strongly Bearish / Negative / Deteriorating
* 0 = Neutral / Flat / Priced-in
* +5 = Strongly Bullish / Positive / Improving

1. Equity Markets: (Outlook for S&P 500 / NASDAQ based on recent flows)
2. Inflation Trajectory: (Are prices cooling? + is Good/Cooling, - is Bad/Heating)
3. Labor Strength: (Hiring and wage reports)
4. Consumer Confidence: (Spending habits and retail sentiment)
5. Forward Guidance: (Fed signaling or general economic outlook)

Output:
Return ONLY a raw JSON object. No markdown, no text.

{
"equities": [integer -5 to 5],
"inflation": [integer -5 to 5],
"labor": [integer -5 to 5],
"consumer": [integer -5 to 5],
